Introduction

The Cuban Missile Crisis was a 13 day politcal standoff that happened between Cuba, the US, and the Soviet Union.

Thesis Statement

In 1962, the situation around the world was The Cold War. In October of 1962, The US sent a spy plane to Cuba because they wanted to see if the rumors about the Cuban missiles were true. When they got there, the spy plane photographed a picture of one of the missiles. This meant that the rumors were true. Shortly after, the Cuban Missile Crisis started and ended. This changed history because it strengthened the US’s relationship with the Soviet Union and U.S citizens were not allowed to travel to Cuba for a very long time. The debate was whether or not to invade Cuba, diplomacy happened when Khrushchev and Kennedy sent letters to each other and signed a peace treaty. The Cuban Missile Crisis change many things like Cuba’s relationships with many countries and countries being scared of Cuba.

Historical Context

The Cuban Missile Crisis was important because it impacted many things. It impacted ideas and culture. The ideas that were impacted were nuclear weapons. The making of nuclear weapons went up after the Cuban Missile Crisis. Culture was impacted because after the Cuban Missile Crisis, US citizens weren’t allowed to travel to Cuba. The Cuban Missile Crisis made people scared of Cubans.
